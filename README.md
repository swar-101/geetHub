## Structure Overview

### 1. physics-of-sound  
The true foundation.  
Waveforms, frequency, amplitude, phase, resonance, harmonics, envelopes, timbre, psychoacoustics.

### 2. digital-audio  
The science behind audio in computers.  
Sample rate, bit depth, aliasing, Nyquist, dithering, quantization, oversampling, noise shaping.

### 3. synthesis  
The art of generating sound.  
Subtractive, FM, wavetable, granular, additive, physical modeling.  
Each section includes notes, screenshots, and mini sound experiments.

### 4. mixing  
Tonal and dynamic balance.  
EQ, compression, saturation, gain staging, imaging, reverb, delay, and headroom.  
Real-world Ableton examples and A/B tests included.

### 5. fx  
Creative effects and vibe engineering.  
Delay, reverb, chorus, flanger, phaser, distortion, micro-shifting, spatial tools, texture FX, modulation tricks, automation sorcery, and creative chains.

### 6. sound-design  
Textures, layers, character, macro programming, racks, presets, sound storytelling, resampling, transient design.

### 7. acoustics  
The environment side of sound.  
Room treatment, standing waves, monitoring setup, psychoacoustic truths, frequency perception.

### 8. mastering  
The final polish.  
LUFS, true peak, multiband compression, limiting, stereo width safety, translation across devices.

### 9. ableton-workflow  
Shortcuts, templates, racks, macro setups, custom devices, and speed-boosting techniques.  
This is my equivalent of IntelliJ sorcery for Ableton.
